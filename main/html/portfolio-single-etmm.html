Project Overview

RiskTracker is a specialized system designed for credit risk officers to simplify ticket management and breach monitoring. It helps quickly identify and resolve breaches, reducing compliance risks and financial exposure. Customizable dashboards make it easy to track progress, prioritize tasks, and manage deadlines efficiently.


Problem Statement

The challenge was to design an application that helps users efficiently manage large volumes of tickets and breaches, ensuring timely SLA compliance. Key pain points included addressing Information Overload and Prioritization Confusion by presenting critical details clearly, while simplifying and Workflow Complexity for different roles. The dashboard needed Real-Time Updates to keep information current without lag, provide actionable insights to solve the Lack of Clear Metrics, and enhance Cross-Team Collaborationfor smooth coordination across teams.

Research

Empathy Map: During the qualitative research phase, I conducted interviews with two user groups—credit officers and risk managers—to gain a deeper understanding of the challenges they face in ticket management and breach monitoring. The empathy map captured key insights into their thoughts, emotions, and frustrations, providing us a solid starting point to shape our solution.

Research

Affinity Map: After gaining insights into the user's thoughts, feelings, and actions through empathy mapping and interviews, we organized and synthesized the data into clusters. This process helped us identify patterns, relationships, and key areas that are common across our user groups.

Ideation

UX Personas: Using insights from user interviews and the outcomes of the empathy and affinity map, we created personas that helped us better understand our users. These personas highlighted their core needs, pain points, goals, and everyday challenges.

Userflow

Using the research findings and personas, I designed a user flow to visually map the application's structure and outline the steps users take to accomplish specific goals. I then presented it to the users, and through discussions, we made several changes and improvements based on their feedback.


Low fidelity wireframes

Using the user flow as a guide, I created low-fidelity wireframes to outline the application's layout and functionality. Shared with stakeholders for feedback, the wireframes prioritized navigation clarity and accessibility to align with user needs.

High fidelity wireframes and User Testing

High-fidelity wireframes were tested with users to validate functionality, enhance navigation, and gather feedback. Insights from testing drove iterative refinements, ensuring a user-friendly design.
